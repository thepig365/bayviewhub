# BayviewHub "Open Your Wall" Landing Page — Soul Brief v2

## 1. What this page is

This page is not a normal gallery content page.
It is not a marketplace page.
It is not a rules page.
It is not a SaaS product page.

It is a **narrative landing page** for a BayviewHub programme that sits **adjacent to** the online gallery and acts as:

- a marketing tool for BayviewHub Gallery
- a higher-quality artwork intake funnel
- a selective private viewing invitation system
- a BayviewHub-mediated bridge between private walls, studios, collectors, and invited viewers

This page must feel like a **campaign / invitation / manifesto with rules**, not a product feature sheet.

---

## 2. What it must inherit from BayviewHub’s live public style

This page must explicitly inherit the current public brand language already visible across **bayviewhub.me** and **gallery.bayviewhub.me**.

### From bayviewhub.me, inherit:

- **calm, destination-led tone**
- **enquiry-first posture**
- language such as:
  - “Enquiries Open”
  - “Create a calm, flexible space...”
  - “Build the Next Chapter with Us”
  - “Creative programs in development”
- a feeling of real place, real space, real invitation
- not loud marketing, not aggressive conversion tactics
- room for future programmes and physical experiences

### From gallery.bayviewhub.me, inherit:

- **enquiry-first viewing-room experience**
- **private viewing by appointment / by enquiry**
- a restrained, curatorial, protocol-conscious tone
- careful legal and positioning boundaries
- a sense that the gallery is selective, not open-access
- a high-trust, low-noise environment

### The combined result should feel like:

**BayviewHub destination calm** + **gallery protocol discipline** + **a more narrative invitation-driven campaign page**

This page should not feel disconnected from BayviewHub. It should feel like the same house speaking in a more campaign-like voice.

## 4. What problem this page is solving

This page must make clear that BayviewHub is solving three real problems:

### Problem 1 — Strong works remain unseen
Many meaningful works live on private walls, in studios, and inside personal collections, but are never seen by the right people in the right context.

### Problem 2 — People want to share selectively, not publicly
Artists, collectors, and private hosts may want to share selected works, but they do not want open-access traffic, casual drop-ins, or public exposure.

### Problem 3 — Serious viewers need a trusted organiser
Curious art lovers, collectors, curators, and invited viewers may want access to more contextual encounters with art, but they need a trusted intermediary, not open access.

### BayviewHub’s answer
BayviewHub becomes the **mediated layer** between work, space, host, and viewer.

## 7. The role of the Preliminary Passport

The Preliminary Passport is not the star.
It is the **record layer** beneath the experience.

It should be introduced only after the relationship logic has been established.

### Correct framing

- The private viewing relationship is the visible programme layer.
- The Preliminary Passport is the supporting documentation layer.
- The QR is the controlled access object, not a gimmick.

### A Preliminary Passport may include:

- artwork image
- title
- artist name
- medium and dimensions
- short context note
- Passport ID
- QR-linked access
- status as a host-submitted private record

Do not overclaim authenticity, provenance, legal title, or valuation.
Maintain the same boundary discipline visible on the gallery site.
